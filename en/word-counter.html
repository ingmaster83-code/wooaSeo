💡 Tips

  • Spaces and line breaks are included in the character count. Use Characters (no spaces) for a closer measure of actual content volume.
  • For SEO, blog posts typically benefit from at least 300–500 words. Longer, in-depth content often ranks better for competitive keywords.
  • Google's search snippets show roughly 120–160 characters of a meta description — keep yours within that range.
  • X (Twitter) allows up to 280 characters per tweet for all languages including CJK characters.
  • Drag and drop a .txt or .md file onto the text area to load it instantly.
How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is estimated based on the average adult reading speed of 200 words per minute. This varies by individual and content complexity — technical or unfamiliar text is usually read more slowly.

How are words counted?

Words are split by whitespace (spaces, tabs, line breaks). Punctuation attached to words is included as part of the word. For languages without clear word boundaries (e.g., Chinese, Japanese), the count may not reflect natural word units — use the character count instead.

Why does word count matter for SEO?

Search engines use content depth as a quality signal. Very short pages (under ~300 words) can be flagged as "thin content." That said, quality and relevance matter more than length — aim for thorough coverage of the topic rather than hitting an arbitrary word count target.

Does this tool save my text?

No. All counting happens locally in your browser. Your text is never sent to any server, making this tool completely private and secure.
