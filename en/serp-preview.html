💡 SERP Optimization Tips

  • Title tag (40–60 chars): Include your primary keyword near the front. Google shows ~55–60 characters on desktop.
  • Meta description (120–160 chars): Summarize the page value and include a call to action. Longer descriptions may be truncated.
  • Google doesn't always use your meta description — it may generate its own snippet based on the search query and page content.
  • Use unique, compelling titles and descriptions on every page to maximize click-through rates (CTR).
  • Check Google Search Console for actual CTR data and impressions for your pages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the exact character count matter for titles and descriptions? ▾
Google's display limit is based on pixel width, not character count. A title with many wide characters (like W or M) may be truncated earlier than one with narrow characters (like i or l). The 60-character guideline is a practical approximation for typical English text.
Why does Google sometimes show a different description than what I set? ▾
Google dynamically generates snippets based on the user's search query and your page content. If Google thinks another part of your page better matches the query, it will use that instead of your meta description.
How can I check my actual SERP appearance on Google? ▾
You can search for your page title or URL in Google directly, or use Google Search Console to see impression and CTR data. The "URL Inspection" tool shows how Google currently sees your page.
Does a longer title always get truncated with "..."? ▾
Yes, if your title exceeds Google's display limit, it will be truncated with an ellipsis (...). Try to keep the most important keywords in the first 40–50 characters to ensure they're always visible.
